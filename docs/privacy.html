Privacy Policy — Heirloom

Honest photo restoration · for your family, not your followers

Effective: June 27, 2026 · Last updated: June 27, 2026

The short version: Heirloom restores one photo at a time. The photo you choose is sent over an encrypted connection to our own server, restored, and the result is sent back to your phone. We do not store your photos, we do not keep a copy, we do not require an account, and we do not show ads or sell anything to anyone. Your photo is processed and then discarded.

This Privacy Policy applies to Heirloom for Android ("the App"), developed by George Abrahamyants ("we," "us," "our").

What the App Does

Heirloom repairs old, torn, faded, or damaged family photos. You pick a photo; the App sends it to our restoration service, which mends damage, restores the face, sharpens, checks that the restored face still matches the original person, and (for black-and-white photos) adds color. The restored image is returned to you. That's the whole product.

What Data We Process

The photo you choose

When you tap to restore a photo, that single image is transmitted over an encrypted (HTTPS/TLS) connection to our restoration backend, which runs on Google Cloud. There it is held in memory only for as long as it takes to process it (about one to two minutes), the restored result is streamed back to your device, and both the original and the result are immediately discarded. We do not write your photos to any disk, database, or log, and we keep no copy after the request completes.

Face-match check (the identity gate)

As part of restoration, the service computes a single numeric similarity score comparing the face in the restored image to the face in your original — this is how the App can warn you if the restoration has drifted away from the real person. This comparison happens in memory during processing. We do not build, store, or retain a faceprint, facial template, or any biometric identifier. The score is shown to you and then discarded with the rest of the request.

Purchase information

If you make the one-time purchase to unlock unlimited restorations, the transaction is handled entirely by Google Play Billing. We receive a purchase confirmation (an entitlement token) so the App knows you've unlocked it. We never see or receive your name, card number, or payment details.

Free-trial count

The App keeps a small counter of how many free restorations you've used. This is stored locally on your device only and is never transmitted to us.

What We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not store your photos or keep copies after processing.
  • We do not require or create an account, and we do not collect your name or email.
  • We do not collect your location, contacts, or device identifiers.
  • We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, analytics SDKs, or advertising IDs.
  • We do not build or store facial recognition templates or biometric identifiers.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with anyone.

Third-Party Services

Google Play Billing

Purchases are processed by Google Play. See Google's privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy.

Google Cloud (our backend)

Our restoration service runs on Google Cloud infrastructure. Your photo passes through it only transiently, in memory, and is processed by software we operate — it is not shared with any third-party AI service. Nothing about your photo is retained.

AI Model Training

We do not use your photos to train any AI model — not ours, not anyone's. Your images are used solely to produce the restoration you asked for, and are discarded afterward.

Biometric Data

Heirloom's identity check is an in-memory mathematical comparison used to warn you about restoration drift. It does not create, store, or use a stored biometric identifier or biometric template. If you reside in a jurisdiction with biometric-data laws (such as Illinois BIPA, Texas CUBI, or Washington's My Health My Data Act), note that we do not collect, store, or process biometric identifiers as defined by those laws.

Children's Privacy

Heirloom is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If you believe a child has used the App in a way that provided us personal information, contact us and we will address it.

Your Rights

Because we don't store your photos and don't hold an account for you, there is no personal profile of you on our servers to access or delete. Specifically:

  • Deletion: Your photos are already deleted automatically after each restoration. To remove all local app data (the free-trial counter and cached files), clear the App's data in Android settings or uninstall the App.
  • Access / questions: Email us at any time and we'll help (see Contact).
  • Purchases: Manage or request refunds for your purchase through Google Play.

Security

  • Photos are transmitted to our backend over encrypted HTTPS/TLS connections.
  • Processing happens in memory; nothing is written to persistent storage or logs.
  • No account means no password or credential for an attacker to steal.

Regional Availability

Heirloom is free to use in Armenia, with no paywall, detected automatically from your Play Store country and device locale. This does not change what data is processed or stored — the same "nothing stored" practices apply everywhere.
